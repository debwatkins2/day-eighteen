Our Ships

Every one of our ships is a unique work of art with its own personality—visit each ship's individual website for more specific information about accommodations.

AMERICAN EAGLE

Captain John Foss
Homeport: Rockland, Maine

"Yes, dreams really do come true" is one passenger's review. "Utter relaxation" Yankee Magazine. "Amazing and memorable" Travel Writers Magazine Tourism Board.

ANGELIQUE

Captain Dennis Gallant
& Candace Kuchinski
Homeport: Camden, Maine

The New York Times sums it up best: Exhilarating! “The thrill of an alfresco escapade” …what’s not to love?

HERITAGE

Captains Doug & Linda lee
Homeport: Rocklin, Maine

"It's like camp for grown-ups" writes Maine Boats Homes & Harbors Magazine. Built by her captains, guests say, "It just gets better."

MARY DAY

Captains Barry King & Jen Martin Homeport: Camden, Maine

According to longtime Boston Globe travel writer Steve Jermanok, Mary Day, is the first sailing schooner built for cruising in Maine.

VICTORY CHIMES

Captains Kip Files & Paul DeGaeta Homeport: Rockland, Maine

"We had such a wonderful time" says Travel Show's Samantha Brown of the largest schooner, Victory Chimes. "They just don't make them like her anymore."

LADONA

Captains Noah Barnes & J.R. Braugh Homeport: Rockland, Maine

Fresh from an extensive rebuild, the Ladona (ex. Nathaniel Bowditch) offers a windjamming experience that hearkens to her vintage pedigree as a 1922 racing yacht.

STEPHEN TABER

Captain Noah & Jane Barnes Homeport: Rockland, Maine

"Rustic luxury, great food!" is how the CBS Sunday Morning crew describes the Stephen Taber. "A beautiful ship and a really spectacular time!"

LEWIS FRENCH

Captains Garth Wells & Jenny Tobin Homeport: Camden, Maine

"So much more than simply a sailing adventure," writes the Chicago Tribune of the Lewis R. French, America's oldest Windjammer and your adventure!
